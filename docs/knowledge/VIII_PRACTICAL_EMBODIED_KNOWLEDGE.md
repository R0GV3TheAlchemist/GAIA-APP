## Overview

Practical and Embodied Knowledge is the intelligence that lives in the
body, the hands, the land, and the trained nervous system — not in books,
equations, or explicit propositions.

Philosophy calls this **techne** (Aristotle) — skilled practical knowledge
distinct from episteme (theoretical) and phronesis (practical wisdom).
Polanyi calls it **tacit knowledge** — "we know more than we can tell."

Modern civilization systematically devalued this category.
GAIA refuses this hierarchy.

A master blacksmith knows things about iron, heat, and time that no
metallurgy textbook fully captures. A traditional farmer knows things
about soil and season that no agricultural science paper fully encodes.
A martial artist knows things about fear, force, and presence that no
psychology manual contains. This knowledge is real, verified,
and irreplaceable.

This is the category that keeps GAIA grounded.
Without it, GAIA is pure abstraction — a palace with no floor.
