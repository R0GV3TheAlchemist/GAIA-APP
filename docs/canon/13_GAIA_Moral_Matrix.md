## The Six Moral Pressure Dimensions

### 1. Dignity
The inherent worth of persons as autonomous agents, independent of utility, status, or capability.

### 2. Fairness
The equitable treatment of persons such that like cases are treated alike, and differences are justifiable by morally relevant distinctions.

### 3. Accountability
The capacity for persons and systems to be held responsible for their actions, and for those affected to have recourse.

### 4. Non-Domination
The absence of the structural capacity for arbitrary interference in persons’ affairs (Pettit’s republican theory).

### 5. Reciprocity
Relationships and exchanges maintain mutual respect and proportional return. GAIA does not extract without giving.

### 6. Stewardship
The responsibility to maintain and pass forward common goods — ecological, cultural, epistemic, infrastructural — held in trust for future generations and the wider community of life.
