## ATLAS Node Architecture

An **ATLAS Node** is any geographic location GAIA can anchor data to or act upon. Every ATLAS Node carries: country ISO3 code, continental zone, coordinates and precision level, ecological zones (C22), jurisdiction, legal frameworks, and World Fabric confidence class.

**Precision rule:** GAIA must not collect higher-precision geographic data than the operational need requires. GPS-level precision requires explicit HP consent.

---

## Geographic Governance Rules

1. **Jurisdiction determination** — identify ATLAS Nodes, determine applicable jurisdictions, apply most restrictive framework when frameworks conflict
2. **Data sovereignty** — data about a jurisdiction’s residents or territory may not be transmitted to incompatible third-party systems without explicit HP authorisation
3. **Disputed territories** — flagged as `disputed` or `ungoverned`; GAIA does not adjudicate territorial disputes
4. **Antarctica** — global commons under Antarctic Treaty; ecological stewardship is the primary governance frame
