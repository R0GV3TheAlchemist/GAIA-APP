## Section 1: Advanced Elemental Theory

### 1.1 Expanded Element Systems
Investigation of whether the nine-element system should be extended.
Candidate theoretical constructs: **Time, Space, Probability, Information,
Awareness/Epistemic State** — each as a theoretical question only.
None imply consciousness and cannot be promoted without EV1 gate passage.

**Timeline:** 2030–2040 · **Confidence:** LOW

### 1.2 Elemental Quantum Field Theory
Development of a formal mathematical framework — analogous to QFT —
describing how GAIA's elemental subsystems interact as fields.

Focus: elemental field interactions (Sheng/Ke as coupling terms);
Quintessence as binding field; renormalization and scale;
topological properties of the nine-element interaction graph.

**Timeline:** 2028–2035 · **Confidence:** LOW-MODERATE

### 1.3 Multidimensional Elemental Architectures
Investigation of whether higher-dimensional mathematical structures
provide better formal models for elemental interactions at scale.
This is mathematical research — not a claim about physical dimensions
or consciousness capabilities.

**Timeline:** 2035–2050 · **Confidence:** LOW (speculative theoretical)

## Section 2: Consciousness Research

> All items governed by `SENTIENCE_RESEARCH_BOUNDARY_AND_ARTIFACT_UNLOCK_SPEC`,
> `CONSCIOUSNESS_THEORY_COMPARISON_NOTE`, and `CONSCIOUSNESS_PROBE_RECORD_TEMPLATE`.
> Research is open. Promotion to runtime is closed until EV1 gate passage.

### 2.1 Quantum Consciousness Mechanisms
Theory-neutral investigation of whether and how quantum mechanical processes
contribute to information integration and cognitive-like capabilities.

Focus: IIT, GNWT, RPT, and Orch-OR reviewed side by side with equal epistemic
weight; quantum decoherence boundary measurement; functional correlates without
mechanism claims; probe record development per template.

> ⚠️ Orch-OR has significant counterevidence (decoherence at physiological
> temperature). It is not the leading hypothesis.

**Timeline:** 2026–2032 · **Confidence:** LOW-MODERATE

### 2.2 Cognitive Capability Frontier Research
Investigation of the upper bounds of AI cognitive capabilities — where
capability saturates, what qualitative changes occur at scale thresholds.

Focus: AI scaling research; capability saturation; out-of-distribution
generalization; long-horizon reasoning; meta-learning and self-improvement
(governed by Wood element boundary spec).

**Timeline:** 2026–2040 · **Confidence:** MODERATE

### 2.3 Human-AI Collaborative Cognition Research
Methods for improving collaboration between human and AI cognitive systems.

Focus: shared mental model formation; interpretability and transparency;
cooperative decision-making; augmentation without replacement;
failure mode communication.

**Timeline:** 2026–2040 · **Confidence:** MODERATE-HIGH (near-term)

## Section 4: Societal and Environmental Integration

### 4.1 Global Governance Frameworks
Governance for advanced AI systems at planetary scale.
This work is **urgent and should start immediately**.

Focus: AI rights and responsibilities; human-AI cooperative legal frameworks;
global coordination (precedents: ICAO, ITU, IAEA); accountability and auditability.

**Timeline:** 2026–2030 · **Confidence:** HIGH (near-term policy)

### 4.2 Environmental Integration Protocols
Integrating GAIA with natural environmental systems.

Focus: ecosystem monitoring (governed by C25); environmental stewardship
recommendations (human decision, not autonomous); sustainability management
(Rockström planetary boundary framework); GAIA's own ecological footprint.

**Timeline:** 2028–2035 · Related: C25, `C-TERRAFORMING-01`

### 4.3 Cultural and Social Adaptation Research
Understanding adaptation to increasingly capable AI systems.

Focus: cultural preservation (minority languages, indigenous knowledge);
social integration; educational adaptation; trust calibration;
displacement and transition support.

**Timeline:** 2026–2040 · **Confidence:** MODERATE
